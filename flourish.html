Flourish
UX Project

Type: School Project | University of Sydney

Role: UX Researcher & Designer

Process: Concepting, User Research, Wireframing, Design, Prototyping

Worked with team of 4 to conduct extensive user research on student engagement at University of Sydney, and used findings to design an augmented reality app that encourages student interaction.

Spring 2017

Context

Over the course of a semester, Henry Lynn, Marco Gallo, Connor Meehan and I were tasked with defining the problems related to social life at the University of Sydney and using the design thinking process to create a proposed solution.





We aimed to address two main themes with our research.





Goals

1. Identify social patterns, preferences, and opinions of USYD students to better understand the relationships users may potentially form through a social play design.

2. Research how campus and the universityâ€™s overall composition are conducive to these social habits, and which areas may be improved to meet user needs.

USYD students expressed dissatisfaction with their university social lives




The Problem

Campus is not conducive to forming new and maintaining old friendships.

Students feel excluded from already established groups and lack a means of meeting new people.

Students lack accessible spots to spend downtime on campus.




Research Methods

Our research consisted of a questionnaire distributed to students throughout campus as well as semi-structured interviews with both domestic and international students. We selected students on campus to be our primary users, and each group member interviewed 2 USYD students about their current social habits at university. We also observed our proposed space for the final design, noting social flow and current use of the area.

Across all our interviews, we were able to define the common traits, goals, and frustrations of students. We collected these points into an affinity diagram.

Categorized data generated from individual interviews

Key Findings

Users feel the social spaces on campus are very congested.

Many users feel disconnected from events and people on campus, citing an inability to access information about university happenings.

Users feel discouraged from approaching groups alone because they assume there are many pre-established friend groups.

Users would appreciate more outdoor spaces for studying and spending downtime.

The Idea

Transform the courtyard into a garden space with plenty of seating and develop a mobile application that rewards the user for social interaction and commitment.

Sketches & Wireframes




Wireframes depicting the process of earning points and spending them on plants




While sitting in the courtyard, the app will prompt students to connect with nearby users. In order to connect, they must introduce themselves to one another and ask for each other's usernames. By encouraging face-to-face interaction, Flourish invites students to make new acquaintances in a low-stress, comfortable environment.



While connected, both students accrue points. The students may talk if they wish, or continue to work or relax on their own. When one student leaves or disconnects, the collection process is halted, and both students collect the loot they earned for that session.





Storyboard explaining the point collection process




Mockup of the garden space.
a) Hedges on the perimetre create a sense of privacy; b) Inward facing tables to facilitate conversation; c) Central walkway increases chance of spantaneous mettings; d) User installed plants create shade and shelter for the area to study; e) Large groups can come together to purchase more substantial trees makes a passively competitive environment




Users can pool points to buy plants for the courtyard. The AR app displays where students aspire to install plants and allows their peers to contribute to their cause. Once a plant reaches sufficient funds, a gardening crew will install its real-life counterpart in the gardens.



Thus, socializing is encouraged through the whole process: gathering resources, planting installations and using the space.
