Stelliform

Worked with team of 7 to build an iOS and Android cross-platform mobile game that features fast-pasted action and strategy with clean, playful design.

TypeSchool project | Cornell University
RoleDesigner
ToolsSketch, Illustrator

Spring 2019

Stelliform is an iOS and Android mobile game that my group members and I developed over the course of a semester. As lead UI designer, in addition to brainstorming game concepts with the group, I created wireframes, mockups, all interface assets, and promotional materials. We released the game at the 2019 GDIAC Showcase and it is available for download on the Google Play store.

The premise

A star has fallen from the sky and the race is on to go claim it. However, the monsters around the area will not make it easy. Players can choose to play as a warrior, rogue, or wizard to blast through enemies by choosing powerful spells or attacks and casting them as quickly as possible while dodging enemy blows to stay alive. Stelliform is a real-time action card game that relies on quick swiping mechanics, split-second decision making, and pre-game planning.



Our team consisted of 5 programmers and 2 designers. While the other designer created character assets, I managed the user interface, all other game assets, animations, and tutorialization.

Development Process

Initial proposal

The process began with many brainstorming sessions and discussions to determine the premise of the game.

Nondigital prototype

We developed and tested a nondigital prototype to see whether players would employ strategy in a fast-paced action game. After testing the game with many users, our key takeaways were that players enjoyed the grid-based gesture system, however we needed to improve the opportunities for strategic decisions rather than blind actions.

Technical prototype

Our technical prototype taught us that adjusting the timing between enemy & player attacks and the number of enemies leads to difficult levels and can drastically change gameplay. This informed our balancing going forward.

Alpha release

By our alpha release, we had implemented all characters, level selection, and card assets. In response to user feedback, we added the deck selection stage to increase the opportunity for strategic planning against enemies.

Beta release

The beta release focused on improving strategic opportunities for players with unique enemy combinations as well as additional effects for spells.
